---Virat Kohli----
Early childhood

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, to Prem and Saroj Kohli. His father worked as a criminal lawyer, while his mother was a homemaker. From a young age, Virat showed signs of being highly energetic and curious. Family and friends often described him as a bright child who was drawn toward sports more than anything else. Growing up in the Uttam Nagar area of Delhi, Virat attended Vishal Bharti Public School, where teachers noticed his strong interest in cricket. Unlike many children who played casually, Virat would often spend long hours practicing batting. His father recognized this passion early and supported him by enrolling him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy when Virat was just nine years old. This decision became the turning point in his life. His family recalls that Virat would carry his bat everywhere and was deeply serious about improving his game even at a young age. He balanced studies with cricket, but the game always held a special place in his heart. The values of discipline, determination, and focus, instilled during his childhood, laid the foundation for the cricketer he would later become.

Start of Cricket journey

Virat Kohli’s formal cricket journey began in 1998 when his father enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy. Under the guidance of coach Rajkumar Sharma, Virat sharpened his technique and developed the aggressive style that would later define him. He quickly rose through school-level cricket and represented Delhi in various age-group tournaments. His breakthrough came in 2006 when he played for Delhi’s Ranji Trophy team, famously batting the day after his father’s death. That courage and dedication marked him as a special talent, setting the stage for his future as one of India’s greatest batsmen.

Milestone Achieved 
2008 - Captained India U-19 to World Cup victory
2008 - Made ODI debut for India
2011 - Part of India’s ICC World Cup–winning squad
2012 - Scored first ODI century vs Pakistan
2014 - Became Test captain of India
2016 - Led India to No. 1 Test ranking
2018 - Fastest to 10,000 ODI runs
2018 - Won all three major ICC awards in one year
2023 - Surpassed Tendulkar with 50 ODI centuries

Virat Kohli’s journey is marked by milestones that reflect his consistency and hunger for greatness. Starting with his U-19 World Cup triumph in 2008, he quickly grew into one of India’s most reliable batsmen. His leadership as Test captain transformed the team into a dominant force worldwide, while his batting records, such as becoming the fastest to 10,000 ODI runs, showcase his skill and discipline. By 2023, surpassing Sachin Tendulkar’s record for most ODI centuries further cemented his legacy as one of cricket’s all-time greats.

Personal Life and Legacy

Beyond cricket, Virat Kohli is admired for his discipline, fitness, and leadership. In 2017, he married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and together they are one of India’s most celebrated couples. He is also a devoted father. Off the field, Kohli runs the Virat Kohli Foundation, supporting underprivileged children and promoting sports. His focus on fitness has inspired a new generation of athletes in India. As a global icon, he has become a role model for young cricketers and fans worldwide, leaving behind a legacy that goes far beyond runs and records.
